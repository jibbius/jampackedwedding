Ceremony

Purpose

The purpose of an Australian Wedding Ceremony is to allow the couple to declare their promises (vowes) to each other, in the presence of their relatives and friends.

What to Wear

Guests can wear whatever they like to our wedding.
Guidelines on what is usually worn are listed below.
Guests can also wear clothing from their own culture if they prefer.

The clothing worn by guests at Australian weddings typically includes -

For Men:

  • Shirt (any color)
  • Suit (any color)
  • Jackets, Ties, and any other accessories are all optional
  • Dress shoe (any color)
For Women:
  • Flexible
  • Typically a knee-length or floor-length dress
  • For outdoor ceremony portion of the day, you might prefer to wear flat shoes (rather than high heels), as the ground at the outdoor venue may include light gravel and/or grass.
  • Guests may choose to wear a different set of shoes or a different outfit in the evening (if they want to).
Please also see the section titled "Australian Weather and Climate", above.

Wedding Reception

Purpose

The wedding reception is a dinner, for us to celebrate the earlier events of the day.

What to expect

Dinner

The dinner consists of a number of courses.
Your meal is brought to your seat
If you have special dietary requirements (for eaxmple - vegetarian, or vegan, or have food allergies, or foods that you do not eat for religious reasons) then you MUST let us know before the day, so that your meals can be prepared accordingly. You cna advise us of dietary requirements via email.

You are free to leave your seat at any time to talk to others, dance, to step outside for smoking or other purposes
In Australia, smoking is not permitted indoors - and outside far enough that smoke does not enter the building.

You can leave your seat at any time to talk to others, dance, or to step outside (for smoking or other purposes)
In Australia, smoking is not permitted indoors - and outside far enough that smoke does not enter the building.

Wedding gifts

The tradition in Australia is that guests can choose to bring gifts to a newly married couple.
This is not required, but if you would like to bring a gift you can do so.

A table will be available at the evening reception where guests can place gifts upon arrival, or at a time of their choosing.
The table also has a 'Wishing Well' where monetary gifts (inside an envelope) can be given.
Gifts are entirely optional, and will not be unwrapped at the event.

Australian Weather and Climate

The climate in Australia during April averages 20°C.
In the evening, the temperature averages 10°C.
Sunset occurs at 6:30pm.
Whilst Geelong is a coastal area, it is not very humid (0 - 10%) and may seem colder as a result.

Rain is a possibility at any time of year in Geelong; whilst we are hopeful that this won't occur during the outdoor ceremony, this is not something we can control.
We have arranged for a sheltered area to be available if required.
In April last year, there were: 19 days without rain - 8 days with negligible rain (less than 1mm) - and 4 days with >1mm rain.

A more accurate prediction should be available in the days prior.

Information about Geelong

Australia is divided into 7 states & territories.
Geelong is situated in the south-eastern state of Victoria.

Geelong is the 2nd largest city in Victoria (200 000 people), which is 60 minutes drive from Victoria's largest city - Melbourne (2 000 000 people).

Australian Currency

The currency in Australia is the Australian Dollar (AUD).

Most stores accept Mastercard and Visa.
Note: Other cards are not widely accepted.

Tourist Tax Refund Scheme

In Australia, a tax of 10% applies to most goods.
If you purchase any expensive items in Australia (more than $300 AUD), and are taking these items back home (overseas), then you may be eligible for a tax refund on these items.

You will need to keep both of the following in your hand luggage when you get back to the airport:

  1. the item
  2. the receipt for the item

You will need to show both of these at the TRS desk, in the Australian airport, immediately after you travel through customs.
The queue can be very long, so you should allow yourself extra time at the airport.

To make the process faster, you can download the TRS app and record your item(s) in the app before you get to the airport.
If you do this, then you can use the shorter (faster) queue.
Do not use the short queue unless you have entered your purchases into the app (when you get to the desk you will simply be sent to the back of the longer queue).

For further information, go to the TRS website.

Laws in Australia

Australian Customs Law

Australia has very strict laws regarding what items you are allowed to bring into the country.
These laws are designed to protect the delicate ecosystem and farming industries.

Items that cannot be brought into Australia include: Plants, Meat, Fruit, Seeds, Clothing/shoes that have been in wilderness areas (wash them first), weapons, and illegal drugs.

You will be required to complete a form on arrival to Australia, and might have your luggage inspected by an Australian Customs official.
If you are unsure about whether specific items in your luggage are allowed, you should ask staff when you land at the airport.

Driving in Australia

For tourists driving in Australia, laws and driving regulations differ from state to state.
In Victoria, you must carry your current foreign driver's licence together with a formal translation of your licence into English.

More information:

  • Driving in Victoria
  • australia.gov.au

The speed limit and road rules are enforced very strictly in Victoria.
Note that you can be issued an expensive fine for traveling even 3km above the speed limit.

Australian Police

The police in Australia are (generally) helpful, friendly, and can be trusted.
However - Police in Australia do not accept cash (or other) bribes.
Trying to bribe a police officer will only get you into more trouble.
